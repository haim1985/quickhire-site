BA Master Training and Weekly Learning Guide

Complete path from assistant-level work toward practical business-analysis support.

Purpose

This guide defines a structured learning and work program to help an administrative assistant grow into a capable Business Analyst. The goal is practical judgment and useful decision support, not theory alone.

What a Business Analyst Does

  • identifies real business problems
  • analyzes regions and markets
  • defines focused pilot projects
  • sets success metrics
  • prepares decision-ready summaries for management

A Business Analyst does not write code and does not design technology.

Learning Stages

  • Stage 1 — Foundations (Weeks 1–4)
  • Stage 2 — Applied BA Skills (Weeks 5–8)
  • Stage 3 — Strategic and Independent BA Work (Weeks 9–12)

Weekly Plan

  1. Week 1: BA Basics — one-page product overview
  2. Week 2: Customer and Stakeholders — customer-pain-value table
  3. Week 3: Problem Definition — two problem statements
  4. Week 4: Executive Summaries — one-page region summary
  5. Week 5: Region Comparison — comparison table and recommendation
  6. Week 6: Pilot Definition — one-page pilot proposal
  7. Week 7: Metrics and KPIs — KPI list with target values
  8. Week 8: Risk and Feasibility — go / no-go recommendation
  9. Week 9: Business Value Thinking — business value summary
  10. Week 10: Executive Communication — executive one-pager
  11. Week 11: Decision Packages — options A / B / C document
  12. Week 12: Independent BA Task — full BA report
